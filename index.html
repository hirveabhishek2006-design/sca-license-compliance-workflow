// overview

Stop reviewing licenses by hand.

Every project pulls in dozens of open-source packages, each carrying its own license — MIT, Apache-2.0, GPL, and plenty of others with terms your company may not accept. Checking each one manually doesn't scale past a handful of dependencies.

This workflow does it for you: it ingests your SBOM, resolves each package's license, compares it against an approved list, and — the moment it finds something restricted — automatically opens a Jira ticket with the exact package, version, license, and a recommended fix. No violation goes unnoticed, and no developer has to go hunting for one.

// architecture

Seven steps, one decision point.

01
Trigger

Runs on a schedule, or manually for testing — no polling, no missed windows.

02
Receive SBOM

Loads the project's dependency list — every package and its version.

03
Scan licenses

Resolves the license for each dependency via Trivy or FOSSology.

04
Compare with policy

Flags each license as allowed or restricted against your approved list.

05
Build summary

Aggregates results — packages scanned, allowed, restricted, overall status.

06
Violations found?

Branches the workflow — clean scans skip straight to the report.

07
Create Jira ticket + report

Opens a ticket with the violating package and remediation steps, then generates the final compliance report.
